What is VeilMetric?

VeilMetric is a research project that predicts your portfolio's future performance using machine learning — and then does the exact same thing on encrypted data, so your financial information is never exposed.

The Two Engines

Standard XGBoost — A conventional ML model that sees your raw portfolio weights. Fast and accurate, but requires trust in the server.

FHE XGBoost (Concrete-ML) — The same model, but running on encrypted data using Fully Homomorphic Encryption by Zama. The server never sees your actual numbers. We compare both to measure the "privacy cost."

What the Numbers Mean

Jensen's Alpha — How much better (or worse) your portfolio does compared to what a basic market theory (CAPM) says it should. Positive = you're beating the market smartly.

Sharpe Ratio — Return per unit of risk. Above 1.0 is good, above 2.0 is excellent. Think of it as "bang for your buck."

Max Drawdown — The biggest peak-to-trough drop. If your portfolio was worth $100k and dropped to $78k at worst, the drawdown is 22%.

Calmar Ratio — Total return divided by max drawdown. Higher means you're being well-compensated for the worst dips.

CVaR (95%) — Conditional Value at Risk. In the worst 5% of scenarios, this is how much you could lose. It's the "nightmare scenario" number.

Δ Delta — The difference between Plaintext and FHE predictions. Smaller deltas mean encryption barely affects accuracy — the core research question.

Model Specifications

Standard: XGBoost (150 trees, depth 6) • FHE: Concrete-ML XGBoost (30 trees, depth 4, 5-bit quantization) • 6 assets over 10yr Indian + Global markets • 4-year rolling prediction window
